Radiography Testing

Radiographic Testing involves placing a sensitive radiographic film beneath a specific component, such as a weld or casting, and exposing it to ionizing radiation (either X-ray or Gamma radiation). The radiation passes through the component, exposing the film beneath it, which results in what is known as a latent image. This image is not visible until the film is developed, at which point it becomes a permanent, fixed image. Discontinuities, such as cracks or lack of fusion within the component, can affect the exposure of the film. Radiographic testing can be applied to most materials, including both ferritic and non-ferritic materials, as well as welds, castings, and often composites. This method provides a permanent record of the inspection and offers high sensitivity in detecting defects.

What are the responsibilities of an individual who is certified to Visual Level 2?

A professional certified to Radiographic Testing(RT) Level 2 has proven their capability to perform Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) in compliance with established UT procedures. Within the scope of competence outlined on their certification, Level 2 personnel may be authorized by their employer to:

  • Select the appropriate Radiographic Testing technique for the test method to be applied.
  • Define the limitations and scope of the Radiographic Testing method's application.
  • Translate NDT codes, standards, specifications, and procedures into practical NDT instructions tailored to the specific working conditions.
  • Set up and verify equipment settings to ensure proper functionality for accurate testing.
  • Perform and supervise Radiographic tests, ensuring procedures are followed correctly.
  • Interpret and evaluate test results based on applicable standards, codes, specifications, or procedures.
  • Prepare detailed written instructions for Radiographic Testing.
  • Carry out and supervise all tasks within or below Level 2 certification.
  • Provide guidance and support to personnel at or below Level 2 certification.
  • Document and report the results of Radiographic Testing, ensuring clarity and accuracy.
