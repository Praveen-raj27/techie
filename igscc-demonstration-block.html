Description

Our IGSCC (Intergranular Stress Corrosion Cracking) Demonstration Blocks are designed to simulate the complex cracking mechanisms associated with intergranular stress corrosion cracking in stainless steel or similar alloys. These blocks provide a reliable and realistic platform for training, equipment calibration, and procedure qualification in Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) methods like ultrasonic testing, eddy current testing, and phased array techniques.
Material Composition: Manufactured using high-grade stainless steel or equivalent corrosion-resistant alloys, representative of real-world service conditions.
Defect Simulation: Precisely engineered to simulate intergranular stress corrosion cracks, mimicking real-world defects caused by corrosive environments under tensile stress.
Heat Treatment: Thermally processed to replicate the intergranular attack and stress corrosion behavior typically seen in industrial components.
