With its versatility and precision, ET is widely used across various industries to ensure the safety, quality, and performance of components.

What is Eddy Current Testing?

Eddy Current Testing uses electromagnetic induction to detect defects in conductive materials. By inducing eddy currents in the material, the technique analyzes the resulting electromagnetic field to identify cracks, corrosion, or other anomalies without causing any damage to the component.

Our ET Services Include:

  • Surface Crack Detection:  Identifying cracks, pits, or corrosion on the surface of conductive materials.
  • Tube and Pipe Inspection:  Evaluating heat exchanger tubes, boiler tubes, and pipelines for defects.
  • Coating Thickness Measurement: Measuring the thickness of non-conductive coatings on conductive surfaces.
  • Material Sorting:  Identifying material properties, grades, and heat treatment conditions.
  • Weld Inspections:  Ensuring weld integrity and detecting weld-related flaws.

Key Features of ET Testing:

  • Non-Contact Method:   Does not require physical contact with the test material.
  • High Sensitivity:  Capable of detecting minute cracks and defects.
  • Real-Time Results:  Immediate feedback for faster decision-making.
  • Versatile Applications:  Effective for complex geometries and thin materials.
  • Portable Equipment:  Ideal for both in-house and on-site inspections.
